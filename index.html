01 — About

A practitioner who designs for the person

My work has always been about meeting people in the middle of change: a young person navigating their first job search, an adult returning to learning after years away, a student stepping into professional practice for the first time. For more than fifteen years, I have worked at the intersection of education, employment, and human development, and what has remained constant across every role is a belief that good learning design begins with deep respect for the learner.

I began my career as an employment counsellor, holding a diploma in social service work, building a practice spanning adult learning, youth programming and community development.

My undergraduate degree in educational studies, with a specialization in adult learning with technology, has given me language and theoretical grounding for what I had been doing intuitively. I now approach instructional and learning design with both practice and principle, using frameworks like universal design for learning, grounding assessment in clearly defined learning outcomes, and drawing on experiential learning theory.

I am drawn to roles in post-secondary education, corporate training and development and learning design where I can contribute to programs and experiences that take equity seriously, built around who learners actually are.
